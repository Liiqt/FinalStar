##### The Stars are Dying, and Something is in the Dark

Every month, it seems another light winks out of existence, or shifts. The Great Dying occurred earlier than scientists predicted it ever would, stars burnt out, collapsed, or went supernova far before even the most pessimistic prediction would call for. And worst of all, it spread with purpose, a pattern of destruction, a wave of death sweeping through star after star, careless of the people around it. But what was worse, even more disheartening than the uncountable trillions lost, was what the survivors told of. A being, something in the void, a warping of light, or a cluster of shining stars that didn't match with any known celestial bodies, no one could give it a name, but one thing was certain: Whatever they were, they were related to the stars burning out.
