## GAR-320 (320 mm Gravity-Assisted Railgun)

**Projectile:**
- **Caliber:** 320 mm
- **Length:** ~1.2 m
- **Rest mass:** 1.8 t
- **Material:** tungsten / DU alloy

**GAID:**
- **Inertial suppression:** ~1:500
- **Effective launch mass:** ~3.6 kg
- **Acceleration length:** ~20 m

**Ballistics:**
- **Muzzle velocity:** 400 km/s
- **Impact energy:** ~1.4×10¹⁴ J
- **Yield:** ~34 kt TNT
- **Effective engagement range:** 10,000–30,000 km

**Energy:**
- **Energy per shot (capacitor):** ~1.3 TJ
- **Shot spacing:**
  - 15 s absolute minimum (destructive)
  - ~40 s sustained
- **Barrel life:** 40–70 shots

**Recoil:**
- **Effective recoil impulse:** ~1.4×10⁶ kg·m/s
- **Ship Δv per shot:** negligible

## H-GAR-800 (800 mm Heavy Gravity-Assisted Railgun)

*Primary use: capital ships, orbital platforms, planetary defense.*

**Projectile:**
- **Caliber:** 800 mm
- **Length:** ~3.5–4 m
- **Rest mass:** 20 t
- **Material:** tungsten / DU composite

**GAID:**
- **Inertial suppression:** ~1:1000
- **Effective launch mass:** ~20 kg
- **Acceleration length:** ~80 m

**Ballistics:**
- **Muzzle velocity:** 600 km/s
- **Impact energy:** ~3.6×10¹⁵ J
- **Yield:** ~860 kt TNT
- **Engagement range:** 50,000–200,000 km

**Energy:**
- **Energy per shot:** ~18–22 TJ
- **Minimum refire time:** ~3 minutes
- **Sustained refire:** ~8–12 minutes
- **Barrel life:** 20–30 shots
